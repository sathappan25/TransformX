How The Math Works

Translation shifts points by offsets: (x', y') = (x + tx, y + ty).

Rotation around a pivot uses trigonometry: x' = x cos(theta) - y sin(theta), y' = x sin(theta) + y cos(theta).

Scaling stretches or shrinks values: (x', y') = (sx * x, sy * y).

Combined transformations are represented with one 3x3 matrix, where order matters.
